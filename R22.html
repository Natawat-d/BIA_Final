Main Argument

AI + BI shifts analytics from reactive to proactive / augmented — systems autonomously detect patterns and suggest optimal strategies, moving organisations away from intuition-driven decisions and static, backward-looking BI reports.

Problem / Context

A data explosion combined with growing market uncertainty means managers still often decide from intuition and rear-view BI reports (consistent with rational and bounded-rational decision models). How organisations actually implement AI-enhanced BI in practice — and how managers perceive it — remains under-studied.

Data & Method

The authors run a qualitative study using in-depth interviews (IDIs) across 20 organisations spanning three economic sectors (services, trade, manufacturing) that actively use BI and AI to support decision-making. Respondents include managers, data analysts, and AI/BI specialists. The objective is to identify the synergy effect between BI and AI — the benefits, the barriers, and managers’ perceptions of the technology.

Key Results

Identified capabilities include automated insight generation, natural-language querying, forecasting, and prescriptive recommendation. Key barriers are data quality, skills shortage, trust, and governance. The paper frames BI as more than a technology — a comprehensive information-management paradigm — with AI-enhanced BI acting as a decision enabler, conditional on organisational readiness.

5 Lessons Learned

  1. BI’s future is augmented analytics: the system proposes candidate strategies, and the manager still disposes (decides).
  2. The core shift is reactive → proactive — from reporting “what happened” to automatically suggesting “what to do next”.
  3. Trust and explainability, not raw model accuracy, are the real gating factor for managerial adoption.
  4. The classic barriers persist across studies: data quality, skills, and governance — the same list that shows up as Big Data’s critical success factors.
  5. BI is fundamentally a management paradigm, not just a technology — AI amplifies it but does not replace organisational judgement.

Exam focus

Exam angle: “How does AI change what BI does for managers?” Answer: it shifts BI from reactive reporting to proactive/augmented analytics — automated insights, natural-language querying, prescriptive suggestions — drawn from 20 real organisations, but adoption still hinges on data quality, skills, trust and governance.
