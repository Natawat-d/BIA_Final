Main Argument

Coupling a crop simulation model with optimization algorithms finds irrigation schedules that cut water use sharply while preserving yield — something neither farmer practice nor an un-optimised simulation model can achieve on its own.

Problem / Context

Water scarcity is severe in semi-arid Morocco, where agriculture consumes roughly 70% of national freshwater. Farmers tend to over-irrigate wheat by habit rather than by any model-based schedule. National cereal production has fallen more than 40%, and average wheat yields sit around only 2 t/ha — making irrigation efficiency an urgent problem.

Data & Method

The study uses AquaCrop-OSPy, the FAO crop-simulation model, coupled with two optimizers: a unidimensional minimize_scalar (bounded) search and a multidimensional L-BFGS-B algorithm. The decision variables are soil-water depletion thresholds, and the objective is irrigation water productivity. The model is applied to two wheat fields in the Haouz Plain (Field 1 water-stressed, Field 2 under normal conditions) across two seasons (2016/17, 2017/18), and calibrated against observed canopy cover, biomass, and actual evapotranspiration (ETa). Three irrigation strategies are compared: Real (farmer practice), NET (one constant depletion threshold), and SMT (stage-specific thresholds aligned to phenological phases — emergence, canopy expansion, max canopy, senescence).

Key Results

Calibration quality is strong: R² 74–93%, NSE 0.71–0.95, index of agreement 0.86–0.98. The optimised SMT strategy cuts irrigation by 26–31% on Field 1 and 42–45% on Field 2 relative to farmer practice, while sustaining yields of 6.43 t/ha (Season 1) and 6.24 t/ha (Season 2) and raising irrigation water productivity to 2.08–2.33 kg/m³. The optimised NET strategy saved only 4–34% — so SMT clearly beats NET on the water–yield balance.

5 Lessons Learned

  1. Simulation + optimization is the textbook prescriptive-analytics pattern: simulate the system, then optimise its decision variables.
  2. The simulation model must be calibrated and validated first (R², RMSE, NSE) — otherwise the “optimum” found afterward is meaningless.
  3. Stage-specific thresholds (SMT) beat one constant threshold (NET) — timing the intervention matters as much as its total amount.
  4. A scarce resource can be cut roughly 30–45% without losing output, because the objective function targeted productivity (output per unit of water), not yield alone.
  5. Prescriptive analytics turns a purely scientific crop model into a farmer-usable decision-support tool.

Exam focus

Exam angle: “Illustrate simulation + optimization with a real example.” Answer: AquaCrop simulates wheat growth under different irrigation choices; the optimizer (minimize_scalar / L-BFGS-B) searches over soil-water depletion thresholds to maximise irrigation water productivity — the stage-specific (SMT) result cuts water 26–45% while keeping yield near 6.2–6.4 t/ha.
